South Carolina opened the SEC Tournament Quarterfinals the same way they have operated all season long, with paint dominance, deliberate ball movement, and a defensive pressure that Kentucky worked to match but could not sustain over forty minutes. What began as a competitive first quarter became a statement game, the Gamecocks pulling away methodically and closing 87 to 64. For four months they have been measured by opponents, pundits, and critics who thought they knew exactly what this program was capable of. Part three of the season begins now, on the biggest stage of the week, and South Carolina's answer is simple: they are beyond measure.

"They think they know who we are, what we're capable of. They've only known what they've seen on game day. This week is when we show that we are beyond measure."
